Introduction

Accurate radiation dose delivery is at the heart of quality external beam radiotherapy treatments and accurate calibration of radiotherapy tereatment units plays an important role in achieving accurate dose delivery. Former type ionization chambers are still held in high regards as the dosimeters of choice for routine calibration of clinical photons and electron beams.

Bellow is a table with information on all the Registered ionization chambers (chambers) used for reference dosimetry at Inkosi Albert Luthuli Hospital. The chambers available on this system are all the chambers that have been registered for mantainance tracking. Chamber mantainance include, Calibration by of the reference chamber by the national standards laboratory as well as in-house calibrations of the field chambers. The in-house cross-calibration is performed using the reference ionization chamber and a Sr-90 check source. The reference chamber is calibrated once every 5 years at the secondary standards laboratory and once a year or when ever the responce of the field chamber is suspected to have changed the reference chamber is used to cross-calibrate the hospital field chambers following recommendations from TRS-398 Code of Practice. When the reference chamber is not in use, it is kept away in a secure environment to avoid incidentces that may change the responce of the chamber.
